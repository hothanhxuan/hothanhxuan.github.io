Power BI

Flight Delays & Network Performance — US Aviation

Power BI · Star Schema · On-Time Performance Analysis

An analytical dashboard processing 5.8 million flight records across 322 airports and 14 airlines. The project dissects On-Time Performance (OTP), delay distributions by cause, and cancellation patterns — revealing which airlines, routes, and time slots are most affected by disruptions.

📅 2024 🛠️ Power BI, DAX ✈️ 5.8M Flights · 322 Airports · 14 Airlines

01. Business Challenges

US aviation data is massive and fragmented — millions of flight records spread across different airports, airlines, and time periods. Without a unified analytical layer, identifying systemic delay patterns and their root causes is nearly impossible.

⏱️ Delay Complexity

Flight delays stem from multiple causes — weather, carrier operations, NAS, security, and late aircraft — each requiring different mitigation strategies.

📊 Scale of Data

5.8 million flight records across 322 airports and 14 airlines need to be sliced, filtered, and visualized to reveal actionable patterns.

🚫 Cancellation Patterns

Flight cancellations were tracked separately from delays, making it impossible to see the full picture of airline reliability.

02. My Approach

I built a star-schema data model optimized for large-scale flight data analysis, with DAX measures designed for time-based aggregation and delay cause decomposition.

1

Star Schema Model

Designed a fact-dimension model with flight records as the fact table, linked to dimension tables for Airlines, Airports, Time (Date/Hour), and Delay Causes.

2

OTP Calculations

Created DAX measures for On-Time Performance rate, average delay duration, delay cause breakdown percentages, and cancellation rates per airline/airport.

3

Multi-Dimensional Dashboard

Built interactive dashboards covering network-level OTP, airline comparison, airport analysis, and time-of-day delay patterns.

03. Dashboard Design

The dashboard provides three analytical views — network overview, airline comparison, and airport-level deep dives — enabling stakeholders to identify problem areas at every level.

04. Key Insights

Analyzing 5.8 million flights revealed clear patterns in when, where, and why flights are delayed:

⏰ Peak Delay Windows

Afternoon and evening flights (3pm–8pm) show significantly higher delay rates — suggesting cascading delays from earlier disruptions accumulate throughout the day.

🌦️ Weather vs Operations

While weather causes the longest individual delays, carrier-related delays are the most frequent — indicating operational efficiency gaps airlines can control.

🏆 Airline Rankings

On-Time Performance varies dramatically across carriers — the gap between the best and worst airlines can exceed 15 percentage points.

🏢 Hub Congestion

Major hub airports (ATL, ORD, DFW) show higher delay rates than regional airports — a direct result of traffic volume and connection complexity.

05. Business Impact

The analysis provides actionable intelligence for airline operations teams, airport authorities, and passengers:

🔧 Operational Focus

Airlines should prioritise reducing carrier-controlled delays (turnaround time, crew scheduling) — the single largest addressable delay category.

📅 Schedule Optimization

Adding buffer time to afternoon/evening flights and spreading traffic away from peak windows can improve system-wide OTP.

🏢 Hub Management

High-congestion hubs need targeted gate management and faster turnaround procedures to prevent delay cascades.
