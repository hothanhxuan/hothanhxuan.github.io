Power BI

HR Analytics: Employee Turnover & Workforce Performance

Power BI · DAX · Workforce Intelligence

I analysed workforce data for ~300 employees to uncover turnover patterns, department-level performance gaps, and engagement drivers. The dashboard pinpoints the Production department's 40.69% turnover rate and proposes targeted retention strategies backed by data.

📅 2024 🛠️ Power BI, DAX 👥 ~300 Employees · 34.78% Turnover

01. Business Challenges

The organization was struggling with a 34.78% overall turnover rate — well above industry benchmarks. HR leadership suspected the problem was department-specific, but lacked the data to prove it or understand why employees were leaving.

Without a clear picture of which departments, tenure bands, and engagement factors were driving attrition, retention strategies were being applied uniformly — wasting budget where it wasn't needed and missing the departments that needed help most.

📊 No Visibility

HR had no centralised dashboard showing turnover by department, tenure, or satisfaction — just spreadsheets with raw headcount numbers.

🏭 Department Blind Spots

The Production department's 40.69% turnover was hidden inside company-wide averages, making it invisible to leadership.

😞 Engagement Mystery

Low engagement scores of 3.39/5 were known, but the root causes — workload, satisfaction, tenure — remained unclear.

02. My Approach

I built an interactive Power BI dashboard that slices turnover data across multiple dimensions — department, tenure, satisfaction, and workload — to identify exactly where and why employees are leaving.

1

Data Preparation

Cleaned and structured employee records including demographics, department, tenure, satisfaction scores, engagement levels, and exit status into a unified analytical model.

2

DAX Analysis

Created calculated measures for Turnover Rate, Average Tenure, Engagement Score, Satisfaction Index, and department-level benchmarks using DAX.

3

Interactive Dashboard

Designed multi-page dashboards with drill-through capabilities — allowing HR to click from company-wide metrics down to individual department and tenure band analysis.

03. Dashboard Design

The dashboard provides two analytical views: a high-level workforce overview for executives, and a detailed turnover analysis for HR managers to identify root causes.

04. Key Insights

The analysis revealed clear, actionable patterns in employee turnover:

🏭 Production Crisis

The Production department has a 40.69% turnover rate — the highest in the company and nearly double the company average.

⏱️ Early Exits

Employees with <5 years tenure are the most likely to leave — suggesting onboarding and early-career engagement failures.

😞 Low Engagement

Average engagement score of 3.39/5 is below target. Departments with lower engagement scores show proportionally higher turnover.

📊 Tenure Sweet Spot

Employees who stay past the 12-year mark (avg. tenure) are significantly less likely to leave — suggesting a retention threshold.

05. Business Impact & Recommendations

Based on the analysis, I proposed three targeted retention strategies:

🎯 Production-First Strategy

Focus immediate retention efforts on the Production department — exit interviews, workload audits, and targeted pay reviews for this high-turnover group.

🤝 Early-Career Programs

Implement structured mentorship and career development tracks for employees in their first 5 years to address the early-exit pattern.

📈 Engagement Boosters

Launch quarterly pulse surveys and manager training to lift the 3.39/5 engagement score — targeting departments below the company average.
