Unlocking E-commerce User Behavior & Conversion Insights

An advanced SQL project using Google BigQuery to analyze Google Analytics e-commerce sessions. Using CTEs, window functions, and data segmentation, the project uncovers conversion bottlenecks, contrasts Purchaser vs Non-Purchaser behavior, and identifies the highest-value traffic sources and product categories.

📅 2025 🛠️ SQL, BigQuery 🔍 Google Analytics Sessions 🧪 Funnel Analysis

Project Overview

This project explores user behavior on an e-commerce platform using Google Analytics data in BigQuery. The main goal is to uncover actionable insights that answer key business questions:

  • Which marketing channels bring the most valuable visitors?
  • How do users navigate from browsing products to completing purchases?
  • What are the conversion rates at different stages of the purchase process?
  • Which products drive the most revenue and which combinations are frequently purchased together?

Case Study 1: Traffic & Channel Performance

The Business Challenge

The marketing team was investing across multiple channels but lacked clarity on which sources delivered the highest-quality visitors. Traffic was growing, but revenue wasn't scaling proportionally — suggesting a disconnect between volume and value.

My Approach

I structured three complementary SQL analyses to map the full traffic picture: monthly trend tracking, bounce rate segmentation by source, and revenue attribution by channel at both weekly and monthly granularity.

Case Study 2: Purchaser vs. Non-Purchaser Behavior

The Business Challenge

Marketing was treating all visitors equally, but clearly not all visitors are equal. The growth team needed to understand: what separates a buyer from a browser? And can engagement depth predict purchase intent?

My Approach

I segmented users into Purchasers (transactions >= 1 AND productRevenue IS NOT NULL) and Non-Purchasers, then compared average pageviews, transaction frequency, and revenue per session using CTEs and FULL JOIN to prevent data loss.

Key Insights

  • Non-Purchasers browse significantly more pages per session — a signal of indecision and friction, not engagement.
  • Purchasers navigate with purpose: lower pageview counts suggest they found the right product quickly.
  • Average transactions per user is ~1.1 — most customers purchase only once, indicating weak retention.
  • Revenue per session data confirms that high-intent users convert efficiently when the right product is found.

Case Study 3: Conversion Funnel & Cross-sell Opportunities

The Business Challenge

The product team knew the funnel was leaking, but couldn't pinpoint where. Additionally, the merchandising team wanted to understand whether customers buying specific products also purchase complementary items — to build bundling and upsell strategies.

My Approach

I mapped the full conversion funnel (Product View → Add to Cart → Purchase) using eCommerceAction.action_type codes and calculated conversion rates at each stage. For cross-sell analysis, I identified co-purchased products using a self-join on buyer visitor IDs.

Key Insights

  • Only ~40% of viewed products are added to cart, and ~10% result in a purchase — the View → Cart transition is the biggest leak.
  • Add-to-Cart Rate improved from 28.47% (Jan) to 37.29% (Mar), a 31% relative improvement — suggesting UX changes or seasonal intent taking effect.
  • 65–72% of users who add to cart never complete purchase — a clear cart abandonment problem.
  • Customers buying specific products also purchase 2–3 additional items on average — strong cross-sell and bundling opportunity.

Conclusions & Recommendations

Key Takeaways

  • Traffic grew from ~60K to ~70K visits (Jan→Mar) but transactions only increased ~10%
  • Bounce rate varies widely: some referral sources >70%, direct/search <30%< /li>
  • Purchasers average ~15–20 pageviews vs. ~5–7 for non-purchasers
  • Average transactions per user is ~1.1 — limited repeat buying
  • Only ~40% of viewed products are added to cart, ~10% result in purchase
  • Cross-sell potential: buyers of specific products purchase 2–3 additional items

Strategic Recommendations

🎯 Optimize Funnel

Improve product pages, UX, and checkout experience to reduce drop-off.

💰 Focus on Quality Traffic

Allocate budget to channels with strong conversion performance.

🔄 Boost Retention

Use remarketing, email campaigns, and loyalty programs.

📦 Leverage Cross-sell

Promote product combinations to increase basket size and AOV.
