Sushi Inventory Management System — F&B Operations

Built a comprehensive Excel-based application to digitize and automate supply chain management for a sushi restaurant in Finland. The system tracks 34 core ingredients across 3 categories through 6,120 daily transaction records, enabling data-driven purchasing decisions and optimized cash flow via automated stock alerts, consumption heatmaps, and supplier performance evaluation.

📅 Jun 2026 🛠️ Microsoft Excel 🍣 34 Products · 6,120 Records 📦 Inventory Management 🇫🇮 Finland · F&B

01. The Business Challenge

Small-to-medium sushi restaurants in Finland face a unique operational challenge: they rely on highly perishable ingredients — fresh salmon, tuna, avocado, nori — that must be tracked daily to prevent costly stockouts during service or food waste from over-ordering.

Most SME restaurants still manage inventory with pen-and-paper logs or basic spreadsheets with no structure, leading to:

🚨 Stockout Crises

Running out of key ingredients mid-service forces the kitchen to remove dishes from the menu — directly impacting revenue and customer satisfaction.

🗑️ Food Waste

Without consumption tracking, over-ordering perishable goods leads to spoilage — a direct hit to profit margins in an industry with razor-thin margins.

💸 Cash Flow Blindness

No visibility into which ingredients consume the most budget, which suppliers are reliable, or when to negotiate bulk purchasing deals.

The goal was to build a zero-cost, no-code solution using only Microsoft Excel — accessible to all staff regardless of technical skill — that transforms daily inventory data into actionable business intelligence.

02. System Architecture

The system is structured as a 7-sheet Excel workbook, each sheet serving a distinct purpose in the data flow — from daily data entry by kitchen staff to executive-level strategic insights for the restaurant owner.

1

Dashboard — Central Control Panel

Bird's-eye view of inventory health with KPI cards, stock status summary for all 34 products, and a monthly consumption heatmap (Green/Orange/Blue).

2

Raw Data — Daily Transaction Log

Single point of data entry for 6,120+ records. Staff enter received, used, and waste quantities — closing stock is auto-calculated. Critical rows are highlighted via Conditional Formatting.

3

Stock Alerts — Inventory Warning Center

Crisis management hub with Red Zone (Out of Stock) and Orange Zone (Low Stock) indicators — designed to be checked at the start of every shift.

4

Monthly Analysis — Trend Analysis

Historical consumption data with H1 totals and monthly averages. Heatmap color coding from Dark Teal (>150 units) to Light Green (<50 units) supports demand forecasting and bulk purchasing negotiations.

5

Product Details — Individual Profiles

Color-coded product cards showing unit, lead time, reorder point, reorder quantity, and 6-month consumption breakdown for each of the 34 ingredients.

6

Supplier Report — Vendor Performance

Evaluates 3 suppliers on Total POs, Total Received Qty, Average Lead Time, and Activity Grade (Active/Steady/Low) — critical for perishable seafood sourcing decisions.

7

Pivot Summary — Executive Insights

High-level diagnostic with category breakdowns (Dry/Frozen/Fresh) and Stockout Health labels — CRITICAL (<5 days), WARNING (<15 days), HEALTHY (≥15 days).

03. Key Features

The system goes far beyond a basic spreadsheet. It's designed as a production-ready business tool with visual intelligence baked into every sheet:

📊 Dashboard KPIs

Professional metric cards instantly show total products tracked, transaction records, alert status, and category count — giving managers a snapshot without scrolling.

🟧 Consumption Heatmaps

Color-gradient maps on both Dashboard and Monthly Analysis sheets — Green for high-usage (best-sellers), Orange for medium, Blue for low (monitor expiration closely).

🔴 Stock Alert Zones

Red Zone: OUT OF STOCK — immediate supplier contact required. Orange Zone: LOW STOCK — add to tomorrow's Purchase Order.

⚡ Auto-Calculated Metrics

Closing stock is computed automatically from opening + received − used − waste. Critical status rows are highlighted in orange via Conditional Formatting — zero manual effort.

05. Role-Based Access Guide

The system is designed for 4 distinct user roles, each interacting with different sheets at different frequencies:

👨‍🍳 Kitchen Staff

Sheet: Raw Data
Frequency: Daily
Action: Enter end-of-shift inventory data — received, used, and waste quantities.

👨‍🍳 Head Chef

Sheet: Stock Alerts
Frequency: Start of each shift
Action: Check for shortages and place urgent orders before service begins.

📋 Store Manager

Sheet: Dashboard, Monthly Analysis
Frequency: Weekly
Action: Plan restocking and review consumption trends to optimize ordering.

🏢 Restaurant Owner

Sheet: Supplier Report, Pivot Summary
Frequency: Monthly / Quarterly
Action: Negotiate with suppliers, optimize costs, and make strategic sourcing decisions.

06. Business Impact

This project demonstrates that advanced analytics doesn't require expensive software. By leveraging only Microsoft Excel, the system delivers enterprise-grade inventory intelligence to a small restaurant operation:

📦 Digitized Supply Chain

Replaced pen-and-paper tracking with a structured, searchable digital system — eliminating data loss and enabling historical trend analysis across 6 months of operations.

🎯 Data-Driven Purchasing

Monthly consumption averages and heatmaps enable evidence-based ordering — reducing both stockouts and over-purchasing by aligning orders to actual demand patterns.

🤝 Supplier Negotiations

Activity grades and delivery performance metrics give the owner leverage to negotiate volume discounts or replace underperforming vendors — especially critical for perishable seafood.
