Power BI

Marketing Spend & Sales Performance — Fashion Retail

Using Power BI and DAX, I integrated 10,500+ marketing and sales records into a Star Schema model to build an analytic dashboard that links campaign spend directly to revenue outcomes. The analysis diagnosed underfunded hero SKUs (Lisa Dress 5 at 43.67× ROAS), identified budget-bleeding campaigns, and proposed a reallocation strategy worth 120–180M VND/month in incremental profit.

📅 2024 🛠️ Power BI, DAX 👗 175 Campaigns · 184 SKUs ⭐ Star Schema 🧠 Design Thinking

01. Business Challenge

A fashion retailer running multiple cross-channel marketing campaigns (both online and offline) had no unified way to measure which campaigns and product categories were actually delivering a positive Return on Ad Spend (ROAS). Marketing budgets were allocated based on historical patterns rather than performance data, resulting in underfunded hero SKUs and overspending on low-performing campaigns.

Management required consolidated insights to answer three critical business questions:

  • Budget Efficiency: How efficiently is the marketing budget being spent across campaigns? Stakeholders needed to understand if allocated budget is generating sufficient engagement at an optimal cost — tracking CPM, CPC, CTR, Budget Usage, Total Spend, and Conversations initiated per campaign.
  • Spend-to-Revenue Link: What is the direct relationship between marketing spend and product sales? Linking top-of-funnel marketing metrics to bottom-line revenue shows which ad variations and product combinations are converting into final orders on the OMS, rather than just generating superficial traffic.
  • Campaign & SKU Optimization: Which campaigns and SKUs should we scale, and which ones are underperforming? Identifying "Star" campaigns guides future budget allocation, whereas discovering underperforming ads/SKUs curtails resource waste.

02. My Approach

To build a practical and effective analytical dashboard, I applied principles from Stanford's Design Thinking process, walking through the problem from the perspective of a Marketing Director and Senior Leadership team:

2

04. Data Preparation & Modeling

1. Data Source Overview

The dataset captures e-commerce operations, product catalog, order transactions, and granular digital marketing performance of a fashion retail company for May 2024. Across the primary tables, the dataset encompasses 10,533 total records:

  • fact-order: 3,450 records — core sales transactions with order details including Customer ID, Product ID, Revenue, Quantity, Discount, and Order Status.
  • fact-mkt_camp_by_sku_cost: 3,981 records — allocated marketing performance at Campaign and SKU level with budget breakdowns and Cost-Per-Result indicators.
  • dim-danh sach san pham (Product Master): 2,249 records — the central product dimension linking sales to marketing with Category, SKU, Selling Price, and COGS.
  • dim-mkt_camp_cost (Campaign Overview): 853 records — top-level campaign information with Campaign ID, Budget, Impressions, Click, CPC, and CPM.

2. Building the Star Schema Model

The Power BI data model follows a Star Schema structure, integrating multiple data sources to enable cross-filtering between marketing efforts and sales outcomes:

  • Fact Tables (Transactional Data):
    • fact-order: Records core sales transactions — Customer ID, Product ID, Revenue, Quantity, Discount, and Order Status. Primary source for Total and Direct Revenue.
    • fact-mkt_camp_by_sku_cost: Records allocated marketing performance at Campaign and SKU level — Budget per product, Amount Spent per product, New Customers, and SKU-level Cost-Per-Result indicators.
  • Dimension Tables (Lookup Data):
    • dim-danh sach san pham: Product attributes (Category, SKU, Selling Price, COGS).
    • dim-mkt_camp_cost: Campaign-level info (Campaign ID, Name, Budget, Impressions, Click, CPC, CPM).
    • dim_date: Standard time-intelligence table (Date, Day, Week, Week_Label) for chronological analysis and WoW comparisons.

3. Table Relationships

The schema connects fact tables to dimension tables via 1-to-many relationships with single-direction cross-filtering, enabling seamless drill-through from campaign metrics to product-level sales outcomes.

06. Key Insights & Recommendations

Key Insights

1. Budget Misallocation — Spending Where It's Easy, Not Where It's Profitable

The company is over-investing in Audrey Shirt campaigns (AOV = 980,000 VND) which have high volume but the lowest per-unit margin in the portfolio. Meanwhile, high-margin products like Percy Set (AOV 1.8M), Delia Set (AOV 1.85M), and Lisa Dress 5 (ROAS 43.67× with only 4.6M spend) are receiving a fraction of what their performance warrants.

2. No Stop-Loss Rules — Budget Bleeding on Underperformers

Campaigns with ROAS <2× continue to run unchecked, burning budget that could be redirected to star performers. One campaign segment ran at CPM 144,314 VND and CPC 43,368 VND — 4.2× the portfolio average CPC — with no automatic halt. Multiple campaigns also show budget utilization >100% (daily budget breached), indicating a lack of automated throttling.

3. Revenue Concentration Risk — Fragility of the "Star" Dependency

The top 3 campaigns (Audrey Shirt variants + Lisa Dress 5) account for a disproportionate share of total attributed Ad Revenue. Audrey Shirt campaigns alone (3 variants) absorbed ~47.6M VND in spend and generated ~637M VND in attributed revenue — representing ~21% of total ad-attributed revenue from a single product line. Any disruption collapses system-wide revenue.

4. The Misalignment Problem — Ad Spend vs. Organic Leaders

Percy Set (138.6M Total Revenue, AOV 1,800,000) has zero ad attribution — selling entirely via Direct Sales. Similarly, Delia Set (96.2M, AOV 1,850,000) and Lilla Dress 3 (92.4M) show minimal ad dependency. The core problem: media budget concentrates on high-volume/low-AOV items (Audrey Shirts at AOV 980,000) while high-margin Sets and Dresses running organically sit underfunded.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Redistribute Budget (20–30%): Shift Audrey Shirt campaign budget toward Lisa Dress 5, Margnet Dress, and Percy Set campaigns. Implement a ROAS-based auto-bidding reallocation model (e.g., if ROAS >10× → increase budget by 30%).
  • Implement Tiered Early-Stop Policy: If daily ROAS <1.5× after 200K VND spend → Pause immediately. If 2-day ROAS <2× → Flag for review and budget cap halved. If CPM >120K for 3+ consecutive days → Auto-stop and report.
  • Build Campaign Redundancy: Maintain at least 2 active ad sets per top-performing SKU, using audience diversification (LAL New vs. Retargeting vs. Interest-based) so no single ad set is a single point of failure.
  • Activate Organic Stars: Launch dedicated ad campaigns for Percy Set and Delia Set to tap high-margin organic demand with paid amplification.
  • Geo-Target 60:40 Hanoi vs. HCMC: Concentrate campaigns in the two metros generating 57.5% of all revenue with differential creative strategy per region.

07. Deep Dive: Campaign & SKU Optimization

🌟 Tier 1 — Scale Immediately

Lisa Dress 5 (HELSDR — Price: 1,550,000 VND)

  • ROAS: 43.67× — the highest performing SKU in the entire portfolio
  • Ad Spend: 4,578,194 VND → Ad Revenue: 199,950,000 VND
  • Total Revenue: 314,650,000 VND | Qty Sold: 203 units
  • Insight: This SKU is chronically underfunded. Every additional million VND invested should return ~43M VND in revenue. Even at a conservative 20× post-scale ROAS, a budget increase from ~4.6M → 20M VND would yield an additional ~300M VND in attributed revenue.
  • Action: Triple the daily budget immediately. Test at 3 audience tiers: LAL New, LAL Expanded, and Retargeting Separate.

(G)Margnet Dress (Price: 1,600,000 VND)

  • ROAS: 17.38× | Ad Spend: 6,537,964 VND → Revenue: 113,600,000 VND
  • Total Revenue: 152,000,000 VND | 95 units sold
  • Insight: Strong AOV of 1,600,000 VND. Total Revenue (152M) exceeds Ads Sales revenue (113.6M), confirming strong Direct Sales demand that ads can amplify without cannibalizing.
  • Action: Add a Retargeting campaign layer. Maintain at least 2 concurrent ad sets.

Audrey Shirt 3 (Price: 980,000 VND)

  • ROAS: 10.52× | Ad Spend: 29,613,977 VND → Revenue: 311,640,000 VND
  • Total Revenue: 318,500,000 VND | 325 units — highest volume in portfolio
  • Note: AOV of 980,000 VND places this in the lower-margin segment (~18–22% margin). Volume play, not premium margin driver.
  • Action: Continue funding but avoid further concentration increases. Use its proven creative as a template for launching higher-margin SKUs.
⚠️ Tier 2 — Action Required

TH 26/4 YOU DESERVE THE MOST BEAUTIFUL THINGS (LAL)

  • CPM: 144,314 VND | CPC: 43,368 VND — the most expensive clicks in the dataset
  • Total Spend: 7,431,381 VND (1.9% of portfolio)
  • Diagnosis: Broad awareness audience with unclear conversion intent. High CPM indicates poor creative relevance score or saturated audience targeting.
  • Action: Pause current creative. Rebuild with product-specific video content. Test a new product angle (testimonial or try-on format). Re-launch with a hard 2-day ROAS floor of 2.0×.

AUDREY SHIRT LAL – Re (Retargeting)

  • CPM: 98,295 VND | CPC: 26,665 VND — 2.5× the average CPC
  • Spend: 23,283,353 VND (5.9% of total budget)
  • Diagnosis: Retargeting audiences are likely over-saturated — same audience retargeted too frequently, causing CPM inflation and diminishing returns.
  • Action: Reduce retargeting frequency cap to max 2 impressions/user/day. Split retargeting into 3-day window vs. 7-day window segments with separate creative.
🔄 Tier 3 — Activate with Ads

Percy Set (Price: 1,800,000 VND)

  • Total Revenue: 138,600,000 VND | 77 units sold | AOV: 1,800,000 VND
  • Ad Attribution: Near zero — selling entirely organically
  • Why it matters: Premium AOV product with proven end-customer demand. If ad spend can achieve even a 5× ROAS, a 5M VND test campaign would yield 25M VND in attributed revenue.
  • Action: Launch a small test campaign (5M VND/month) with LAL New audience. Focus creative on lifestyle positioning, not product shots.

Delia Set (Price: ~1,850,000 VND)

  • Total Revenue: 96,200,000 VND | 52 units | AOV: 1,850,000 VND — highest AOV in top-15
  • Ad Attribution: Minimal
  • Action: Ideal candidate for a premium "style editorial" content campaign. Target Hanoi and HCMC audiences aged 25–35 with interest in fashion/lifestyle.

🔬 Micro Case Study: TH 25/4 ELEGANT AESTHETICS — The Pausing Mistake

  • The campaign was actively running with a tracking ROAS well above the 5× threshold.
  • Campaign was manually paused by the operations team (likely interpreting a temporary CPM spike as underperformance).
  • Impact: Revenue dropped the following days as the campaign's warm audience lost momentum and needed to be re-built from scratch.
  • Key Lesson: Never pause a campaign based on a single-day CPM spike without reviewing 3-day rolling ROAS. Implement a mandatory 48-hour cool-off review before any manual campaign pause.
