Power BI

Strategic BI & Market Growth Dashboard — SuperStore Global

Power BI · Star Schema · Market Expansion Analysis

A comprehensive Power BI solution using a fact-dimension data model to process 51,000+ transaction records. The dashboard tracks $12.64M in revenue with an 11.61% profit margin — revealing profit pillars, loss-making SKUs, and high-potential expansion zones like Canada.

📅 2024 🛠️ Power BI, DAX 📊 $12.64M Revenue · 25K Orders

01. Business Challenges

SuperStore Global operates across multiple product categories and geographic markets — but leadership had no unified view of which products, regions, and customers were actually driving profit versus silently destroying margin.

Revenue growth of +51.5% YoY looked strong on paper, but buried underneath were loss-making categories, a 4.68% return rate eroding margins, and no clear data to guide regional expansion decisions.

📉 Hidden Losses

The "Tables" category was causing a net loss of $64,083 globally — but this was invisible in top-level revenue reports.

🌍 Expansion Blind Spots

No data-driven framework existed to evaluate which regions offered the best profit potential for expansion.

🔄 Return Rate Impact

A 4.68% return rate was eroding margins, but no one could pinpoint which products or regions were driving returns.

02. My Approach

I built a multi-page Power BI dashboard with a fact-dimension star schema model, using DAX measures to surface actionable insights across four analytical dimensions: executive overview, market performance, product profitability, and regional deep-dives.

1

Data Modelling

Structured 51,000+ transaction records into a star schema with fact tables (Orders, Returns) linked to dimension tables (Products, Customers, Geography) for fast, predictable queries.

2

DAX Measures

Created calculated measures for Profit Margin %, Revenue Growth YoY, Return Rate, and Customer Lifetime Value — enabling dynamic slicing across all dimensions.

3

Multi-Page Dashboard

Designed four focused dashboard pages: Executive Summary, Market Analysis, Product Analysis, and Canada Insights — each answering specific strategic questions.

04. Key Insights

The dashboard revealed critical patterns that were invisible in traditional spreadsheet reporting:

4.1 — Profit Pillars vs Loss Makers

✅ Profit Pillars

  • Technology: 13.99% profit margin — the highest-performing category
  • Office Supplies: 13.69% margin — consistent, reliable profit driver
  • 178K total units sold across all categories

❌ Margin Eroders

  • "Tables" category: net loss of $64,083 globally
  • Specific SKUs like "Chromcraft Bull-Nose Wood Oval Conference Tables" losing >$1,800 per order
  • Bulky Furniture & Appliances dragging down US Central margins

4.2 — Canada: The Hidden Champion

Canada emerged as the highest-margin market thanks to a high-margin product mix and natural avoidance of loss-making furniture categories. This finding directly supports a case for prioritised expansion into Canadian markets.

05. Business Impact

The dashboard transformed how leadership makes strategic decisions — replacing gut feelings with data-backed evidence.

🎯 Boost "Star" Categories

Prioritise marketing spend for Technology and Office Supplies — the two categories driving the bulk of the $1.47M profit.

🔧 Restructure Margin Eroders

Discontinue or reprice loss-making SKUs in the Tables category to immediately recover $64K+ in annual losses.

🌍 Expand into Canada

Use Canada's high-margin success as a blueprint for regional expansion — replicating its product mix strategy.

👥 Customer Loyalty

Focus on high-value customers (e.g., Justin Ritter) with targeted loyalty programs to maximise lifetime value.
